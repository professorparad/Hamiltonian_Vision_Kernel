Hamiltonian Vision Kernel

A spatially aware hybrid reconstruction pipeline that combines image patches, tensor-network features, positional encoding, variational quantum observables, and Hamiltonian energy regularization.

Sparsho Chakraborty and Siddhartha Patra

School of Basic Sciences, Department of Physics, Indian Institute of Technology Bhubaneswar; Centre for Quantum Engineering, Research and Education (CQUERE), TCG CREST.

MPS patch features Fourier positions HVK1D and HVK2D CIFAR and Mona Lisa benchmarks

Architecture Flow

1

Image Patches

The input image is converted to grayscale and split into local patches.

2

MPS Compression

Each patch is summarized through tensor-network features from SVD-style structure.

3

Position Encoding

Patch coordinates are encoded with sinusoidal features so spatial context is preserved.

4

Quantum Kernel

A variational circuit produces X, Z, and correlation observables for each patch.

5

Patch Decoder

A decoder reconstructs pixels while Hamiltonian energy shapes the learned representation.

Model Variants

HVK1D

Uses a one-dimensional Heisenberg-style interaction pattern over nearest-neighbor qubit correlations. The standard and symmetric versions are available in the CIFAR baseline suite.

HVK2D

Uses a two-dimensional grid interaction pattern, making the latent order parameters closer to spatial image structure.
